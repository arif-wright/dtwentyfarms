# Crop experience

Future customer stages: Reserved → Harvest confirmed → Seeded → Germinating → Under the lights → Nearly ready → Harvested → Packed → Ready for pickup → Collected.

Customers normally follow shared crop batches connected to their order items, not “their tray.” A dashboard may show the upcoming harvest, order/payment state, pickup date/pass, latest approved update, assigned varieties, batch codes, seeded dates, stage, estimate, photos, and grower notes. Orders include receipts, history, reorder, and refund status. Preferences cover varieties, notifications, and pickup. Harvest passes expose credits, application, skips, and expiration.

Operator tools should derive real work from paid reservations and crop plans: today’s tasks, trays required with loss buffer, rack/shelf positions, stage changes, photography, packing, courier flags, and collection. Later, measure actual yield, costs, waste, labor, and margin. No fake metrics or customer-facing progress should be generated.

Photos require operator approval, batch/order authorization, safe metadata, and storage policies. Customer visibility ends when the assignment relationship does. Private pickup details appear only for an authorized paid order during an appropriate time window.
