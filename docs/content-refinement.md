## Reduce repetition

The homepage, shop, harvest detail, how-it-works page, FAQ, and terms repeat the threshold and fulfillment mechanics. Repetition is currently safer than ambiguity, but it lengthens mobile pages and makes the brand feel operational. Establish canonical reusable policy content before live sales, then use:

- a one-sentence summary on the homepage;
- a compact outcome panel beside checkout;
- the full explanation on How It Works/FAQ;
- precise contractual language in Terms.

## Make the offer more appetizing

Once verified, add sensory language grounded in the actual crop rotation: crisp stems, peppery radish, mild broccoli, sweet pea shoots, cut-to-pack timing, color, and suggested pairings. Avoid health promises, nutrient multipliers, certification language, or claims not supported by the growing and handling process.

## Remove before public launch

“Phase 1,” “preview release,” “demonstration content,” “checkout links have not yet been configured,” and form/email technical notices are appropriate during development but make a public storefront feel unfinished. Keep them while features are disconnected; remove or replace them only when real dates, checkout, forms, and policies are operational.

## Voice principles

Use warm first-person operational clarity where it helps (“Michael will email you after orders close”), concrete food language, and short sentences. Keep D20 language in names and small details. Avoid turning limitations, automation plans, database concepts, or production architecture into marketing copy.
